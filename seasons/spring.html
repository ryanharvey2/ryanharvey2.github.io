Allegro

Spring has arrived with joy
Welcomed by the birds with happy songs,
And the brooks, amidst gentle breezes,
Murmur sweetly as they flow.

The sky is caped in black, and
Thunder and lightning herald a storm
When they fall silent, the birds
Take up again their delightful songs.

Largo e pianissimo sempre

And in the pleasant, blossom-filled meadow,
To the gentle murmur of leaves and plants,
The goatherd sleeps, his faithful dog beside him.

Allegro

To the merry sounds of a rustic bagpipe,
Nymphs and shepherds dance in their beloved spot
When Spring appears in splendour.

Spring
(Concerto No. 1 in E Major)

Spring opens The Four Seasons with a sense of joy and renewal. The first movement, Allegro, bursts with energy, featuring trills and ascending lines that mimic bird calls, capturing the liveliness of the seasonâ€™s rebirth. Vivaldi uses light, playful melodies to depict brooks flowing and birds singing, blending the natural beauty of springtime into music. In the Largo e pianissimo sempre second movement, the pace slows, portraying a goatherd resting beneath a tree as his dog barks nearby, evoking a gentle scene of pastoral calm. The final Allegro movement closes with lively dance rhythms, celebrating the rejuvenation of life with shepherds celebrating in the fields.
